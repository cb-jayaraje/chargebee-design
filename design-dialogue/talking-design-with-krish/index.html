Design Dialogue

Talking Design with Chargebee’s CEO, Krish Subramanian

Krish is the CEO and a co-founder of Chargebee. Shaping the product and vision over the past 8 years, his perspective on design has grown and evolved. We caught up with the man himself to learn about this evolution.

“Business applications can no longer
afford to be boring.”

As a young startup, Chargebee did not have a design team onboard. Most design decisions were taken by Krish and other co-founders, and they created designs with a little help from interface designers.

With time, however, it became clear that design was beyond the visual. Chargebee’s Design team today boasts of dedicated Product Design, Communication Design, UX Writing, Research, and Operations teams.

Krish shared his thoughts on design and why a design-driven organization fuels an exemplary customer experience.

What inspired you to think of a design team for Chargebee?

According to me, I don’t think there was a need or a want. It’s essential. For me, this began when the perspective of design slowly changed from mere visual design elements to something beyond that. During our earlier days, Rajaraman handled most of the interaction design, while also wearing the hat of a product manager. In time, we realised that we needed to involve a team of design thinkers.

What is great design as you see it?

When something is so good, it will not seek attention. It will blend seamlessly with the interface. A "wow" moment for me is not just about the visual elements, it’s definitely beyond that. Let me give you an example. When I was using an Apple iPod around the year 2006, the clicking noise it made every time I was scrolling or trying to adjust the volume piqued my interest. This extremely tiny detail could have been forgotten,

but instead it added a fitting auditory touch to the experience. Great design is paying attention to even the smallest of details.

When something is so good, it does not seek attention.

Do you believe that design impacts business? Can you explain how?

It would be absurd not to believe that, partly because a generational shift has already happened. These days if you don’t have good design, it’s going to stand out and seem weird. A business application cannot afford to be boring; it has to be consciously concerned about what the users want to achieve. Great design is when the users don’t have to interact at all.

Your thoughts on "Form over function"?

What matters the most is if the customers are feeling at ease and are comfortable with Chargebee's user experience.

What are the applications or websites where the experience made you go “wow”?

Definitely Pocket. Once I copy a URL and open the application, it automatically asks me if I want to bookmark it.

This is super cool because the application knows my context and what exactly I want to do with the URL. Similarly, I love my Google Inbox. If for any reason I forget to add an attachment, it reminds me to attach the file. So I always feel like it has my back.

If for any reason I forget to add an attachment, it reminds me to. So I always feel like it has my back.

What’s your vision for Chargebee and how critical is design to it?

“Design is a separate discipline. Just like sales, marketing, and customer success”.

Design is a separate discipline just like sales, marketing, customer success, and engineering. From the very start, i.e. all the way from promise to delivery, design plays a crucial role. So design is responsible for the translation of business needs to the eventual expression of how to solve that particular need.

In terms of design, where do you see us when compared to our greatest competitors?

InVision and Stripe are pushing benchmarks in design standards. From a design perspective, we must aim to achieve best-in-class design. From a business perspective, the design should focus primarily on customer experience and not the competition.

The Chargebee design experience then and Chargebee design experience now — what have we lost and what have we gained?

What we've gained is better user empathy. There is a need for the design team to interact closely with customers. The team has evolved beyond visual design by involving UX researchers, focusing on interaction design, and increased user research aspects of visual thinking. Also, I feel the organization has increased efforts to hire people who are specialists in their areas to create an impact. This is the tradeoff we must strive to arrive at.
